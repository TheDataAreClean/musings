title: Writing in Public

Most things I write are finished before anyone reads them. The essay exists as a private object — drafted, revised, put away, retrieved, revised again — until at some point it tips over into done and gets sent somewhere.

This is not that kind of writing.

## The Difference

Writing in public, in the sense I mean, is writing that is visible at every stage of its incompleteness. A blog post published while still half-formed. A note that is obviously a note. A thought that has not yet decided what it is.

{% marginnote %}
There is a version of this that is pure performance — the writer who "thinks out loud" in a way that is very carefully composed. This is not what I mean, and I suspect it is obvious when it is happening.
{% endmarginnote %}

The risk is embarrassment. The gain is a different kind of honesty — writing that is still moving, still finding out what it believes, in a way that finished writing cannot be.

## What Gets Lost in Finishing

When you finish something, you choose. You decide what the piece believes. You cut the paths that went nowhere. You make the argument cleaner than the thinking that produced it.

This is skill. It is also a kind of loss.

The mess of arriving at an idea contains things that the clean statement of the idea does not. The wrong turns were not simply wasted — they were part of finding out where the right turn was. They have information in them that the final direction does not.

Public writing at the half-formed stage preserves some of this. The arriving is still visible.

## Things that help

- Write in units small enough to be honest. A paragraph can be true in a way a chapter cannot.
- Date things. The date is an excuse for imprecision — this was true *then*, which is not the same as a claim that it is true *now*.
- Resist the urge to hedge everything. "I think" and "perhaps" and "it seems to me" are useful, but used too freely they become a way of avoiding the commitment that makes writing worth reading.
- Publish before you are ready. The gap between ready and published is often just anxiety performing as diligence.

This post is an example of the thing it describes. I am not sure it is finished. I am publishing it anyway.
